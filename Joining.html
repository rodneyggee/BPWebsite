Joining BP Medical Aid Society

Only full time employees of BP Southern Africa (Pty) Ltd are eligible to join BP Medical Aid Society as a principal member.

About Joining

Only full time employees of BP Southern Africa (Pty) Ltd are eligible to join BP Medical Aid Society as a principal member.

Membership can be continued in the by the principal member if they leave the employment of BP South Africa in certain situations.

Dependants who are not employed by BP South Africa can choose to continue membership under certain conditions.

The Medical Aid year

Benefit limits are allocated in calendar year cycles (from 1 January to 31 December). After which they reset for the following year. Most benefits follow a one year cycle. When a new member joins, some of the benefits limits will be adjusted prorated.

General Waiting Periods

Most recently, the applicant has not had medical aid coverage for a period of more than 90 days and prior to that had not had previous medical continuous coverage period of at least 24 months. Then they can be applied up to a period of 3 months when the membership can not claim except for PMB conditions.

Condition Specific Waiting Period

For pre-existing conditions, waiting periods may be applied up to 12 months depending on Policy underwriting.

Existing Pregnancies may be applied up to 9 months.

Applications with HIV, if you are already registered on the LifeSense HIV Programme you are excused from waiting periods.

Late Joiner Penalties

If a Member or Dependant joins a Medical Aid and they are over the age of 35 and there has been gaps in their medical aid coverage history, the scheme can legally increase their contributions.

BPMAS not charge late joiner penalties if the member joins the society from the date of their employment.
